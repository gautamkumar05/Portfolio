Fleet management Dashboard

Fleet management Dashboard

Introduction:

This Power BI dashboard presents a Fleet Summary Report for August. The data is visualized through various metrics and charts to offer a comprehensive view of the transport activities for the month. Let's break down each section:

Key Metrics:

Total Trips of August: 2552
This is the total number of trips conducted throughout August.
Avg of Day: 85 The average number of trips per day is 85.
Max of Day: 139 The maximum number of trips on the busiest day was 139.
Min of Day: 3 The lowest number of trips recorded on the least busy day was 3.

Outcome:

Top 5 Transporters By Trips:

This bar chart shows the top 5 transporters in terms of the number of trips they completed: Simran Enterprise: 291 trips
Garg Logistics: 259 trips
Singh Coal: 233 trips
Kauleshwar Infra: 227 trips
NA: 204 trips (this could represent unknown or unclassified trips)

Risky Level: The pie chart breaks down the risk levels of the trips:
Low Risk: 67% (1698 trips)
High Risk: 33% (854 trips)
Total Trip By Transporter: A more detailed table representing the trip count for all transporters: Simran Enterprise remains the top transporter with 291 trips, followed by others.

Seal Report: This table presents the Seals Report (related to cargo or trip validations):
Matched trips (no mismatch): 2480 trips Minor mismatches (20 in total):
Matched = 19, Mismatch = 1 (20 trips)
Matched = 14, Mismatch = 6 (1 trip)
Matched = 12, Mismatch = 6 (1 trip)
Matched = 8, Mismatch = 12 (1 trip)
Zero matched trips: 44

This highlights the integrity of the trips based on seal matching, where most trips are matched without issues.

Day Wise Trip: The line chart visualizes daily trip count fluctuations. Some notable days: The peak of 139 trips occurred around the middle of the month. There were days as low as 3 trips, demonstrating variability in daily operations.

Route Summary: A table showing routes and their corresponding trip counts:
Haldia port to Jamuria: 767 trips, the most frequent route.
Other major routes include:
Amrapali to Jamuria: 448 trips
OMC to SMEL Sambalpur: 259 trips
Magadh to Jamuria: 250 trips
Magadh to Mangalpur: 152 trips

Conclusion: The Fleet trip summary for August shows efficient management with a total of 2552 trips, averaging 85 trips per day. Most trips are low-risk (67%), and only a small percentage have seal mismatches (less than 1%). The busiest routes and transporters are clearly identified, with Simran Enterprise leading in transport services and Haldia port to Jamuria being the busiest route. Operational fluctuations are evident, but overall the trips appear well-managed. The risk and seal report data provide insights for further improving transport security and consistency.
